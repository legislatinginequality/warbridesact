79 S. 563

February 19, 1945

Sen. James William Fulbright (D-AR)

A BILL Declaring to be citizens of the United States certain spouses of United States citizens serving honorably in the armed forces during the Second World War.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled, That the Nationality Act of 1940, approved October 14, 1940 (54 Stat. 1137), is hereby amended by adding, immediately following section 312 thereof, a new section to be numbered 312A and reading as follows:

“SEC. 312A. Any alien racially eligible to naturalization who is now or may within one year after the termination of hostilities in the present World War be married to a citizen of the United States who has served honorably in the armed forces of the United States after December 7, 1941, and before such termination is declared to be a citizen of the United States as of the date of the marriage: Provided, That the foregoing shall not apply to the spouse of a citizen whose marriage is judicially annulled retroactively to the date of marriage without such spouse having taken up residence in the United States.
