79 H.R. 2650

March 16, 1945

Rep. George Gregory Sadowski (D-MI)

A BILL Declaring to be citizens of the United States certain spouses of United States citizens serving honorably in the armed forces during the Second World War.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled, That the Nationality Act of 1940, approved October 14, 1940 (54 Stat. 1137), is hereby amended by adding, immediately following section 312 thereof, a new section to be numbered 312A and reading as follows:

"SEC. 312A. Any alien racially eligible to naturalization who is now or may within one year after the termination of hostilities in the present World War be married to a citizen of the United States who has served honorably in the armed forces of the United States after December 7, 1941, and before such termination, shall become a citizen by taking the oath of allegiance to the United States prescribed by section 335 of the Nationality Act of 1940 (54 Stat. 1157; 8 U.S.C. 735). Such oath may be taken before any court of the United States or of any State authorized by law to naturalize aliens or before any consul of the United States. Certified copies of oaths taken before the courts shall be sent to the Immigration and Naturalization Service. Certified copies of oaths taken before consuls shall be sent in duplicate to the Department of State, one copy of which shall be forwarded by that Department to the Immigration and Naturalization Service. The judicial annulment of any such marriage retroactively to the date of the marriage shall render the naturalization, and any document issued in evidence thereof, subject to revocation on a proceeding in accordance with section 338 of this Act if the naturalization oath was taken before a court, or under section 340, if such oath was taken before a consul of the United States.["] [sic]
